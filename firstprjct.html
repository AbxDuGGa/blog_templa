Trials of the Ring
As far as I remember, since I was a kid, my parents forced me to learn martial arts. My first ever sport was Kyokushinkai Karate, it’s the combat sport that teaches you patience, endurance, and durability. I was there for almost a year, and then I moved on to Judo. That was the first ever big journey in my life. I have a lot of precious memories of it, every moment whether it’s sad and bad, or wonderful and mature were priceless for me, it lasted for 5 years. The last phase for now of my combat sport journey was- Box, and MMA. I fell in love with both of them by the first seconds I stepped in ring and octagon.
The Weight I Carry
I’m from a big family; I have 3 beloved little brothers and 2 beautiful little sisters. My mother always told me that I, as an oldest son and child, should take care of my little ones. So, for more than half of my life I spent time with them. I always try to do my best, even if I’m not perfect, I still want to be good role model for them, so they could look up to their older brother.
The Silent Obsession
I used to play chess every day, as it helped me sharpen my mind, think strategically, and stay patient—qualities that later supported me in both sports and life. I was literally a chessholic for a long time; I played chess everywhere. It all started when I lost to my friend. At that time, I couldn’t play properly, and I just thought, why can’t I play better? So, I started watching chess matches on YouTube, Instagram, and other platforms. Within just six months, I became better than many of my friends.
